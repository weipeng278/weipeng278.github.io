Precision Medicine is different than the current, “one-size-fits-all” method for health care. Precision medicine is health care that is based on you as an individual. It takes into account factors like where you live, what you do, and your family health history. The goal of precision medicine is to be able to tell people the best ways to stay healthy. If someone does get sick, precision medicine may help health care teams find the treatment that will work best. Precision medicine is not currently available, but if a million or more people participate, new approaches to keeping everyone healthy will be made available.
